After doing our second set of interviews, we discovered new feedback that could be directly applied to our app.

One feedback was refining the "poke" or matchmaking process. It seems like it is ambiguous on how the feature works, how it pairs people, and seems confusing and unreliable to users, so it should be made more transparent and have further developed tags/filters.

Another was generally fixing up the UI. There were bugs, what was described as an unnatural flow, and overall just not as clean. It was simple though.

A big resonating opinion was having a group chat feature, much like for the matchmaking process. Users felt like there wasn't a reliable way to communicate for a pre-scheduled event, and think it would make meeting up much more reliable and successful.

Users seemed to like how simple the UI was, the icons, creating meetings, and the overall app layout.

Overall, it seems like we need to rework the matchmaking system, fix current systems in place through bugfixing and testing, make a more intuitive UI design, and look into making a chat towards scheduled events. It seems the theme, artistic aspects, and event scheduling parts of our app were successful.
